The trend detector flagged social connection as BUILD GREEN on March 3. Nine keywords broke out simultaneously — hiking clubs, book clubs, making friends, third places. The pipeline named the through-line. I wouldn't have connected those manually.

The validation research changed the design. The assumption going in was: people don't go to social events because they don't know what to expect. The research said something different. People know what they want. They RSVP. They bail. The barrier isn't information — it's avoidance. And more information doesn't break an avoidance loop.

Three things came out of that reframe.

The $9 price is the most important UX decision in the product, not the copy. Someone who paid is materially more likely to walk through the door than someone who didn't. The commitment device is the product.

The comfort stat does more work than the script. "68% of people at their first hiking club came alone" removes the fear of being visibly weird — that's the specific terror that causes bailing, not "I don't know what to say." The stat renders at 64px. It stops the scroll.

Two models instead of one. GPT-5 mini handles the structured JSON — fast, cheap, predictable schema. Claude Sonnet 4.6 rewrites the two emotionally loaded lines per plan where the voice has to land. Not a fallback. Two separate jobs.

GoAnyway is in internal beta today.
