Most birthday gifts are forgettable. Gift cards are basically cash with extra steps. Amazon same-day is "I thought of you 40 minutes ago." People want to give something that feels personal. They just don't have the time — or usually the skill — to make it.

That's the gap. A song written *specifically about someone* — their obsession with hot sauce, the time they got lost in IKEA, what their dog's name says about them — lands differently than any product you can buy. You can't Amazon Prime that.

## What shipped

- The product concept is locked: conversational intake → Claude generates custom lyrics + Suno style string → founder manually generates on Suno V5 → song delivered via a shareable link + email
- The intake is a chat, not a form — the differentiator is that it feels like writing a love letter, not filling out a bureaucratic field
- The target user is named: impulse gifters. Not planners. The mom who remembered at 10pm. The friend who saw the birthday post on Instagram 20 minutes ago
- Price set at $9.99. Fast enough that guilt turns into action before they talk themselves out of it
- Stack decided, brand locked, scaffold shipped to [songfor.gift](https://songfor.gift)

## Why

Every competitor asks for a name and a genre. That produces generic output. The real IP here is the prompting methodology — the system for translating personal details (quirks, jokes, memories, relationship dynamics) into lyrics and Suno style strings that produce emotionally resonant songs. That's the moat. The song people forward to their friend and the friend actually cries — that's what makes this a product and not a feature.
