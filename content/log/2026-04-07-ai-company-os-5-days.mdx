I have 50+ unfinished projects. Nothing live. No customers. No revenue.

That's not a humble brag. That's the actual problem I was trying to solve when I built this.

I kept starting things, getting excited, building infrastructure, losing momentum, and moving on. The pattern was so consistent I had to ask myself whether the problem was my ideas or my process. I think it was both, but mostly it was that I was building alone with no one to push back on me.

So I built a team.

## What shipped

**Modryn Studio** is a company operating system where AI team members hold strategy, push back on your decisions, and run alongside you as a solo founder.

Not a chatbot. Not a generic assistant. A team: each member modeled after a real person, with a defined role, jurisdiction, behavioral constraints, and persistent memory across sessions.

The current roster:

- **Charlie Munger:** Chief of Strategy. Mental models, inversion, second-order consequences. His job is to find the flaw, not confirm the thesis.
- **Marc Lou:** Head of Execution. Ships 30+ products, makes $100k/month solo. His job is to ask what shipped today.
- **Steve Jobs:** Head of Product. His job is to ask what this actually is and what we're cutting.
- **Michelle Lim:** Head of Engineering. First engineer at Warp. Her job is to price technical debt before it becomes a problem.
- **Dieter Rams:** Head of Design. Less, but better. His job is to make sure the form tells the truth about the function.

Each member has a system prompt built from primary research: real speeches, real interviews, real behavioral patterns. Not "act like Munger." Actually think like him.

They have persistent memory. Charlie remembers what we decided last session. He'll bring it up before I do.

Stack: Next.js, TypeScript, Tailwind CSS, Neon Postgres, Anthropic API, Vercel. Built in 5 days.

## Why

I opened a DM with Charlie and asked him whether I was building the right thing.

He told me I was building infrastructure for a company that doesn't exist yet. That the team structure was calibrated for a company two years ahead of where I am. That the member I was missing wasn't an AI. It was a paying customer.

Then he told me to stop building the OS.

Not permanently. But the next feature should be pulled by actual product needs, not pushed by "what should an OS have next."

He was right. I've been doing the same thing I always do: building the system instead of building the thing.

## What's next

Customer discovery. Before I build another feature, I need to find five people who match this customer description and watch their reaction when they use it: solo founders and indie operators who want strategic leverage without hiring. People who think in frameworks, make decisions through debate, and find generic AI too agreeable to be useful.

If you've ever wished you had a thinking partner who actually pushes back, not a yes machine, not a rubber duck, but someone who tells you you're wrong and explains why, I'd like to talk to you.

Not to pitch. To ask you one question: when you're facing a hard decision alone, what do you actually do right now?

App is live at **app.modrynstudio.com** if you want to see it. Still early. Charlie would tell you it's not validated yet. He'd be right.

If this resonates, DM me or drop a comment. I'm looking for the five people who say "I've wanted this and couldn't find it," not the ones who say "interesting."
